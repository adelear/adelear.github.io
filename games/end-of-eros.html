End of Eros

Action · Networking · Unity Photon · C#

Role: General Programmer, Intro Cinematic Artist | Year: 2023 - 2024

Platform: PC Executable | Genre: Multiplayer Action | Art Style: Stylized 3D | Target Audience: Everyone

Overview: End of Eros is Top-Down Combat Multiplayer game, where I collaborated with over 20 students as a programmer a 3D top-down online battle arena made with Unity. I worked as a programmer focused on multiplayer systems, gameplay flow, and player onboarding. I developed the round manager to sync team scores and manage game states, enhancing multiplayer gameplay Created tutorial room for improved player onboarding and crafted game’s intro cinematic to establish the narrative Successfully showcased End of Eros at the 2024 Toronto FanExpo and CNE, highlighting teamwork and technical accomplishments to large audiences

Gameplay: Brawl against your friends and foes in this top-down combat arena fighting game. Choose your champion - possessing unique abilities, and discover your ideal play style. But you better hurry, a desert storm is brewing, limiting areas you can run or hide. Will you conquer the arena?

My Contributions

  • Multiplayer and Game State Systems
    I implemented a round manager system responsible for synchronizing gameplay flow across all connected clients. This system ensured consistent team score tracking, correct transitions between gameplay states, and reliable synchronization across networked players using Unity's Photon Fusion. This improved stability and reduced edge-case desynchronization during multiplayer sessions.
  • Player Onboarding and Tutorial Design
    To improve first-time player experience, I designed and implemented a dedicated tutorial scene that introduced players to the core movement and combat mechanics, player abilities and energy usage, as well as match flow and objectives. This allowed players to learn the mechanics in a controlled environment before entering matches, improving accessibility and player retention.
  • Intro Cinematic
    Wrote, drew, and edited the intro cinematic for End of Eros, establishing the game's narrative context. This cinematic was presented upon game startup, and used during public showcases and events to communicate the game's identity to new players. The project was successfully showcased at Toronto FanExpo and the CNE (2024), where I helped present the game, explain systems to players, and gather live feedback from large public audiences.

Tools & Workflow: Unity, Photon Engine, 3DS Max, Substance Painter, Photoshop, Procreate, Clip Studio Paint, Trello, Discord

Highlights

  • Multiplayer Arena Combat
    Modular component-based architecture designed for team collaboration. Scriptable Objects are used to define abilities, stats, and prefabs, as well as clean separation of input, combat, movement, and state logic enables rapid iteration and balancing.
  • Multiplayer Arena Combat
    Real-time online multiplayer built around short, high-intensity matches, with team-based rounds accompanied with synchronized scoring and win conditions.
  • Modular Combat System
    Combat built from interchangeable systems: movement, offense, defense, and energy. Supports melee and ranged playstyles with distinct risk–reward tradeoffs. Additionally, there are status effects, knockback, invincibility frames, and cooldown management are core to moment-to-moment decision-making
