Labinac’s Cuisine

Role: Programmer, Tech Lead 4-dev team | Year: 2024

Platform: Web | Genre: Restaurant Simulation | Art Style: Low-Poly Stylized & 2D Cartoon | Target Audience: 12+

Overview: Labinac’s Cuisine is a quirky underwater restaurant simulation game. Players manage a struggling eatery for fish patrons, experimenting with cooking and serving dishes. When ingredients run out, creative (and dark) solutions are required — fitting the TFS Game Jam 2024 theme, Fool. Labinac's Cuisine won first place in the 2024 TFS Winter Game Jam.

Gameplay: Navigate the underwater restaurant, prepare and serve meals, and keep customers happy. Fish have AI-driven behaviors, and timing is key. Your choices affect the final Food Inspector rating, determining whether your restaurant succeeds or fails.

Core Features:

  • Cooking: Experiment with ingredients to feed fishy patrons.
  • Serving: Fast-paced drag-and-drop mechanics for timely meal delivery.
  • AI Customers: Fish have unique behaviors, hunger timers, and satisfaction metrics.
  • Multiple Endings: From failure to perfect acclaim based on your decisions.

Tools & Workflow: Unity, 3DSMax, Substance Painter, ProCreate, Photoshop, Trello, Discord

Highlights

  • Deceptive Narrative Design Tied to Core Mechanics
    What begins as a traditional restaurant simulation gradually subverts player expectations as ingredient scarcity forces morally questionable solutions, embedding the game jam theme directly into gameplay.
  • Physics-Inspired Drag-and-Drop Interaction System
    All interactable objects inherit from a shared Draggables base class, enabling consistent movement, collision boundaries, and contextual feedback across kitchen tools, fish, and food items.
  • State-Driven AI Customers with Timed Behavior
    Fish patrons operate on a clear state machine with independent timers that pause, resume, or terminate based on player interaction, creating dynamic yet readable customer behavior.
  • Strategic Resource & Quality Management
    Different fish types apply weighted modifiers to food quality, encouraging players to balance speed, cooking precision, and ethical compromise to optimize final ratings.
  • Multiple Endings Based on Emergent Play
    Player performance feeds into a final Food Inspector evaluation, producing five distinct endings that reflect service quality, efficiency, and moral decisions.
  • Scalable Script Architecture via Inheritance
    Core gameplay systems were designed for rapid iteration under game jam constraints, emphasizing code reuse and extensibility through inheritance-based architecture.
